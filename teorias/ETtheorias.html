Scientific Theories

What are Scientific Theories: They are elaborate proposals which aims to make us reflect on what we are, where we came from, and where we go. A set to a very detailed explanation of a phenomenon.
Theories represent the best explanations of various phenomena, but please keep in mind that they are always attempts and not conclusive, are not absolute truths or dogmas.

One theory well accepted these days may well be modified or discarded; with new techniques new studies, new observations and experiences, a theory can be completely modified, discarded, or increasingly accepted in the scientific means.
Theories are useful primarily for its predictive values. The theory-based predictions are tested with new experiments. Theories that make predictions and agreed with success are generally well accepted by the scientific community.
